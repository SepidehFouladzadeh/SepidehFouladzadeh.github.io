Understanding Complex Systems by Borrowing Perspectives Across Disciplines

Notorious for jumping between topics. To me, though, they've been filling in the same coherent picture, driven by questions rather than disciplinary boundaries :)

My curiosity is less about collecting knowledge across disciplines than about understanding how different ways of thinking relate to one another. Every field offers a different perspective on the same landscape, and I'm fascinated by what becomes visible only when those perspectives are brought together.

Drawn to understanding the same underlying questions through multiple lenses. From well-understood dynamical systems to messy biological and human systems, I’m interested in understanding the principles that carry across domains.

A common thread running through many of my explorations: How can we understand, influence, and reason about complex systems that we only partially understand?

About

Following Curiosity Across Borders and Disciplines

Still building my understanding through many failed attempts—each one a step toward understanding how complex systems behave.

A rough but rewarding transition from Mechanical Engineering to Bioengineering— from systems like autonomous vehicles to proteins and biological networks. Different systems, yet all revolving around the same fascination: dynamical systems—anything that changes over time, where we aim to understand, and perhaps eventually shape its behavior.

Broad explorations so far— from mechanistic modeling to data-driven approaches, from deterministic dynamics to probabilistic reasoning, and more recently toward integrating prior knowledge with multimodal data.
