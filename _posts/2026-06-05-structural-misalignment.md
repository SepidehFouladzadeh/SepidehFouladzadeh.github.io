An often underappreciated reality of institutions. An incomplete account of human structures. 

Often deemed structures of coordination. Rarely merely organizational, but emotional architectures where direction, evaluation, and protection are never evenly distributed.

Built on asymmetric bonds of dependence. The guided and the guiding. One still in formation. Another entrusted with shaping the conditions of that formation.

Multiple authorities, competing sources of direction and future. 

And perhaps inevitably, a lack of coherence. Rarely remaining where originated. Traveling, delegating the burden to the dependents. 

No longer an assumption that authority speaks with one voice. 

And beneath them, one still developing a sense of self. A child in growth. A scholar in formation.

The vulnerable dependent, the absorbent layer of contradiction, the meeting point of incompatible expectations, and the convergence of conflicting obligations. 

Caught between separated worlds, carrying the burden of reconciliation. Expected to reconcile fractures they did not create, tensions they cannot fully resolve, and dynamics that were never theirs to carry.

A divided dependence, and the strange experience of becoming, unintentionally, a reminder. 

Patterns of thought, or simply existing that evoke the presence of the other. Carrying traces of one world into another. A foreign element. No longer encountered purely as oneself, but as a resemblance.

The confusing duality of being welcomed and resisted simultaneously. Indecisive of whether to integrate you or protect from what you represent. Like an implant never fully synchronized with the “host”. 

An awareness that support has become conditional. A subtle pull toward allegiance. And gradually, a selective suppression of the self. Becoming less activating, less reminiscent of other worlds.

A deep irony. 

Development receding into the background. A displacement of attention, from formation to accommodation.

And perhaps a hidden architecture. Not only of what is taught, but of what remains unreconciled, and of where its burden eventually settles.
