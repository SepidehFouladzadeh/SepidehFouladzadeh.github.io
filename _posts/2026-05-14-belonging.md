Roaring a departure, and on the hunt for an appropriate arrival.

An initial feeling of expansion, flooded with visions of new versions of the self— only to encounter an unexpected face of identity: A fragile equilibrium, quietly destabilized by transitions.

A temporal trajectory toward a new steady state. A chronic sense of interrupted belonging and ongoing becoming. An in-between existence. Unanchored from the previous, unintegrated into the next. No longer fully of the old, and not yet of the new.

From an elevated sense of belonging— to more places and more people, to a gradual descent into half-belonging. A temporary suspension of identity. Uncertain, yet performing composure.

Wearing the “transitioner” tag as a badge of courage— signaling flexibility, adaptability, and resilience. To some, an asset. To others, a source of caution.

Meeting a promising new arrival, only to notice an unexpected emergence of unease: An undercurrent of alertness. A readiness for reconfiguration. A learned detachment for survival.

Seemingly, a prolonged transient— perhaps revealing the beginning of never-ending transitions. 

Landings stamped by an expiration date, takeoff already imagined at the moment of landing. No champagne moment left with arrivals. A shadow of departure embedded at arrival.

A persistent tension between ambition and nostalgia. A hope for taking root again, and a concern for a residue— a memory of prior attachment.

Alarming.

A temporary non-belonging, quietly evolving into a dilution of ever belonging.

And the question lingers: Is growth worth the erosion of belonging?
