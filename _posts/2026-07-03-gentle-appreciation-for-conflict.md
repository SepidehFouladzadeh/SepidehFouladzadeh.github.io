Perhaps the most unsettling realization about human conflict. Rarely the simple meeting point of good and evil. Not even, by necessity, the absence of good intentions.

Yet, all, human in the end, somehow colliding at times, encountering one another through the shape of their defenses.

And the word *conflict*, perhaps still waiting to be defined.

Perhaps merely a phenotype—the observable manifestation of hidden dynamics.

And conflict resolution, perhaps so often falling short, quietly calling for the black box of human interactions to be understood more mechanistically.

And perhaps the debut of a different definition.

A perturbation, momentarily breaking equilibrium, offering another clue toward reconstructing the hidden network of human nodes that lies beneath.

Perhaps not a phenomenon to be explained, but the means by which a fragment of human experience becomes more explainable.

Perhaps neither to be avoided nor merely resolved, but approached with enough curiosity to infer the hidden architecture giving rise to observable behavior.

And perhaps the goal has never been the absence of conflict, but conflict that remains informational—a temporary, chaotic simulation carrying the possibility of returning not to the equilibrium that once was, but to one that could never have existed without it.
