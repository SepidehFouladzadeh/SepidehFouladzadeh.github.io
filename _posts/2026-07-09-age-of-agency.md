Our familiar promise of a linear path toward independence, and the implicit bargain of following another’s lead long enough to finally become independent. 

Following long enough, proving long enough, obeying long enough, and eventually, perhaps, independence.

A bargain seemingly designed for an era of information asymmetry, for a different landscape of scarcity, one in which access itself was the rarest resource. To knowledge, to opportunity, to reputation.

Perhaps a future increasingly saturated with agency. Increasingly self-generated visibility, increasingly democratized means of creation, and increasingly decentralized influence.

Perhaps marking a new paradigm of leadership—not of coordinating labor, nor of coordinating knowledge, but of coordinating agency.

And with it, perhaps, a different form of exchange.

No longer seeking permission to participate in the world, but companionship that compounds ambition.

Aligning not out of dependence, but around shared adventures.

Perhaps hierarchy, an increasingly outdated necessity.

Or perhaps not hierarchy itself, but the signals through which it earns legitimacy.

Signals that can no longer be appointed, but must be earned continuously.

Perhaps through every judgment, every decision, every act of stewardship.

And perhaps through a vision worthy of voluntary surrender.
