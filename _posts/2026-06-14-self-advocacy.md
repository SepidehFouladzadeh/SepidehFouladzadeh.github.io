Self-advocacy or migration. Perhaps one of the most timeless dilemmas of human development.

A deep ecological incompatibility, or perhaps merely the playground of an information deficit. An atmosphere of partial observability, curable through an added dose of clarification and articulation. 

Convincing a declining supporter, reactivating belief in a hesitant gatekeeper, pleading for belief in a potential difficult to debate. 

And a grief in recognizing the limits of an ecosystem without villainizing it. 

Augmenting observers to expand innate perceptual capacities, updating priors to refine estimates, only to present reality a bit more faithfully.

A continued attempt to advocate for a potential that has not arrived in a familiar form. A value increasingly requiring translation, and a translation that is not free.

Shared finite resources for explaining legitimacy and becoming. A resource competition between translation and formation. A seesaw between expansion and survival.

A constant battle of proving legitimacy, and a new character of self-advocacy. No longer a brief clarification, but an ongoing negotiation. 

A leakage of energy. 

An ecosystem that was intended to metabolize growth gradually begins to downregulate it.

An organism appearing mismatched to the soil, and the appealing logic of seeking a more compatible ecology. An optimization appealing only in theory, concealing the assumption of an adjacent nourishment. 

A departure offering no guarantee of compatibility elsewhere, only exposure. Migration not toward abundance, but toward extinction.

Perhaps not ending with the organism, but revealing a responsibility carried by the soil.

Not to downgrade ecological compatibility, but whether the soil, having once accepted responsibility for its nourishment, continues offering all it can rather than withdrawing support for the organism to self-select out.

Especially when growth is still being earnestly pursued, when the branch carries a future bloom, a future not yet in season.
